Syllabus

DISCRETE-TIME MARKOV CHAINS: Classification of states, Stationary distribution, reversibility and convergence. Random walks and electrical networks. Collision and recurrence.
Law of large numbers Different modes of convergence and their relations. Weak Law of large numbers, First and Second Borel-Cantelli Lemmas, Strong Law of large numbers (proof under finite variance).
Central limit theorem Characteristic functions, properties, inversion formula and Levy continuity theorem, CLT in i.i.d. finite variance case. Slutskys Theorem.
Introduction to Finite Markov chains Definition. Random mapping representation. Examples. Irreducibility and aperiodicity. Stationary distribution and reversibility. Random walks on graphs.

References and Texts

  • [D] Probability: Theory and Examples by Rick Durrett.
  • [LPW] Markov Chains and Mixing Times by David A. Levin, Yuval Peres, and Elizabeth L. Wilmer.
  • [N] Markov Chains by James Norris.
